Film: Walking Through a Divided Chinatown

The prompt for our first individual assignment in Film Production class was “A Day in the Life,” and my project documents a day walking through Boston’s Chinatown. Beginning with the development of two interstate highways in the 1950s, which destroyed the center of the community and displaced over a thousand residents, Chinatown has become increasingly gentrified by high-income housing units and the expansion of Tufts University. Amidst the division, I wanted to show the vitality of the neighborhood, because Chinatown is such an important center for its immigrant residents and the broader Asian American community.
